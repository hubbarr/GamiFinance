Investment Facts

Text about investments and about why we chose to look at stocks and bonds

  • Stocks
    • Share in the ownership of a company.
      • Not actually own a corporation but own the share issued.
    • Corporate property legally separated from the property of the shareholders.
      • Limits liability of both the corporation and the shareholder.
    • Issued by companies to raise capital in order to grow the buisness or undertake new projects.
    • Riskier investments than bonds
      • Bondholders paid before shareholders in bankruptcy.
    • Can enjoy returns generated by increasing profits, theoretically to infinity.
    • Historically returns between 8-10% on intial investment.
    • Common stock
      • Great majority of stocks issued in this form.
      • Claim of profits (dividends) and confer voting rights.
      • Variable dividends that are declared by board of directors, never gaurnteed -- often not paid out at all.
      • Most often get one vote per share.
    • Preferred Stock
      • Similar to bonds
      • Usually no voting rights
      • Investors usually gaurnteed a fixed dividend in perpetuity
      • Paid off before common shareholders (but still after debt holders/creditors).
  • Bonds
    • Companies can issue to raise money instead of borrowing directly as a loan from a bank.
    • Bondholders are entitled to interest as well as repayment of principal.
      • Bond holders = creditors to the corporation
  • Creditors are given legal priority over other stakeholders in the event of a bankruptcy.
  • Historically returns 5-7%
